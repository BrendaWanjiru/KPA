Event 1: Neonatology

Date: June 5, 2024

Location: Central Park

Description: World Pneumonia Day: Your Role in Ending the Silent Epidemic Webinar

Neonatology is a subspecialty of pediatrics that focuses on the medical care of newborn infants, particularly the ill or premature newborn. It is a hospital-based specialty and is usually practiced in neonatal intensive care units (NICUs). The principal patients of neonatologists are newborn infants who are ill or require special medical care due to prematurity, low birth weight, intrauterine growth restriction, congenital malformations, sepsis, pulmonary hypoplasia, or birth asphyxias.

Event 2: Gastroenterology

Date: July 20, 2024

Location: City Stadium

Description: Management of Acute and Chronic Diarrhoea

Gastroenterology is the branch of medicine focused on the digestive system and its disorders. Diseases affecting the gastrointestinal tract, which includes the organs from mouth into anus, along the alimentary canal, are the focus of this specialty. Physicians practicing in this field are called gastroenterologists. They have usually completed about eight years of pre-medical and medical education, a year-long internship (if this is not a part of the residency), three years of an internal medicine residency, and three years in the gastroenterology fellowship.

Event 3: Infectious Diseases

Date: August 15, 2024

Location: Downtown Art Gallery

Description: World Pneumonia Day: Your Role in Ending the Silent Epidemic Webinar

Infectious diseases are disorders caused by organisms such as bacteria, viruses, fungi, or parasites. Many organisms live in and on our bodies. They're normally harmless or even helpful, but under certain conditions, some organisms may cause disease. Some infectious diseases can be passed from person to person. Some are transmitted by bites from insects or animals. And others are acquired by ingesting contaminated food or water or being exposed to organisms in the environment.
